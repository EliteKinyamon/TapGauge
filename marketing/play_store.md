## Full description

**TapGauge: for the tank sensor RVers already know is lying to them.**

If you own an RV, you know the problem: the factory fresh, grey, and black tank
gauges are almost never right. Residue coats the internal probes and they read
"2/3" for weeks, or jump straight from full to empty. Aftermarket sensor kits
promise a fix — but they cost $100+ and mean installing new hardware on your rig.

TapGauge takes a different approach. **No sensor to buy or install — just your
phone.** You tap the side of the tank with a knuckle, and TapGauge listens to
the sound with your phone's microphone. The pitch of a tank changes with how
full it is (the same reason a bottle's note rises as it empties), and TapGauge
turns that pitch into a fill percentage.

**How you set it up (once per tank):**
- **Fresh water:** tap right after you fill, and again when the pump runs dry.
- **Grey & black:** tap "Just Dumped" at the dump station for your 0% point, then
  do a one-time **Driveway Calibration** at home with clean water — add water in
  known amounts (counted jugs or a timed hose), tapping after each step. Dump and
  rinse before your trip. (Yes: clean water, on purpose, once. It's the only way
  to learn the in-between levels without waiting months.)

**Why RVers keep it:**
- No extra hardware, no $100+ sensor kit, nothing to wire in.
- Works completely offline. No account, no sign-up, no internet permission at all.
- Your recordings never leave the phone and are never saved as files.
- Gets more accurate the more you calibrate; shows an honest confidence level.

**Honest about limits:** TapGauge is a convenience estimate, not a certified
gauge. It needs a quick per-tank calibration, and big temperature swings can
shift readings (the app nudges you to re-calibrate when that happens).

Stop guessing. Stop trusting a sensor you know is wrong. Tap your tank instead.
